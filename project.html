Predicting Passenger Survival on the Titanic

The Titanic disaster on April 15, 1912, was one of history's most infamous shipwrecks, claiming 1,502 lives out of 2,224 passengers and crew due to insufficient lifeboats. The challenge involved building a predictive model to determine which types of people were more likely to survive the tragedy. Using passenger data such as name, age, gender, and socio-economic class, the goal was to uncover patterns and answer the question: “What sorts of people were more likely to survive?”

Data Professional Survey Analysis

This Power BI dashboard analyzes responses from over 630 data professionals to uncover global trends in the industry. It highlights insights on job roles, salary ranges, preferred tools, and career satisfaction. Python emerged as the most widely used language, while Data Scientists reported the highest salaries and students the lowest. Notably, 42% of respondents found it easy to break into the field—challenging common perceptions.

Netflix-TV-and-Movie-Show-Analysis

This Power BI project analyzes Netflix’s global catalog to uncover content trends through visual exploration of genres, release years, and durations. The dashboard reveals that movies make up over 70% of the platform’s content, with Drama being the most common genre. 2015 marked a significant spike in releases, reflecting a key growth phase. Runtime analysis shows most movies follow the standard 90-minute format.
